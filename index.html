About

Floriane Kaneza was born on March 12, 1987 in Bujumbura, Burundi. She did Communication Studies at Hope Africa University and a specialization in “Inspirational Leadership” at H.E.C Paris. Her interest goes around Cinema and Events, and her profile highlights an independent filmmaker and a Cultural Activist. Floriane Kaneza is the Co-founder of PEPCinéma, an international non-Governmental Organization aiming to strengthen the film culture. It focuses on Kids and young people, and brings a support to young filmmakers by taking their works to the international scene via distribution. Floriane Kaneza is the Festival Director of the URUSARO International Women Film Festival, a project by CinéFEMMES RWANDA. She is a mentor in Global Give Back Circle for MasterCard Foundation Scholars Community Platform.
